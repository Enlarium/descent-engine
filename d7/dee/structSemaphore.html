Detailed Description

A counting semaphore.

A semaphore maintains a non-negative count representing the number of available resources. Threads may decrement the count by waiting on the semaphore, or increment the count by signaling it.

Semaphores do not have ownership semantics:

  • Any thread may signal a semaphore.
  • The thread that signals need not be the one that previously waited.
Note
This semaphore is intra-process only and cannot be shared between processes.
Warning
Semaphores do not provide mutual exclusion guarantees. Using a semaphore as a mutex is discouraged; use a lock like Mutex, Qutex, or RWLock instead.
